Chapter One

Why Wood?

In a world of fast furniture, understanding what makes a material honest — and lasting — is your most powerful sales tool.

Walk into any furniture store today and you'll find tables made of MDF, chipboard, or printed foil. They look like wood. They're priced like a bargain. But give them ten years, a spilled glass of water, or a house move — and they will not survive.

Solid wood is not simply a premium choice. It is the logical choice. It is the only furniture material that improves — actually becomes more beautiful — through use and time. The patina that forms on an oak dining table isn't wear. It's a record of a life lived around it.

This is your opening argument. Not price justification. Not brand prestige. The simple truth: solid wood furniture is the last furniture you'll ever need to buy.

"The question isn't why solid wood costs more. The question is: how much does cheap furniture cost you over twenty years?"

Three materials define the furniture market today: solid wood, MDF/chipboard composites, and upholstered frames. Each has its place — but only one will outlast the customer who bought it.

A new dining table being prepared at the Ethnicraft atelier, Indonesia.

Retailer tip: When a customer hesitates at price, break it down. A solid oak dining table at €3,000 that lasts 40+ years costs €75 per year. An MDF alternative at €900 that needs replacing in 8 years costs €112 per year. Solid wood is actually the economical choice.

Chapter Two

The Wood Manual

Ethnicraft masters four species of solid wood. Each has a personality. Knowing them is knowing your product.

Click each species to explore its character — from origin and hardness to how it ages in a customer's home.

Oak +

European White Oak · Quercus robur

The backbone of Ethnicraft. Strong, characterful, and endlessly adaptable.

European White Oak is the cornerstone of the Ethnicraft range. Grown slowly in sustainably managed forests across France, Germany, and the Balkans, it develops a tight, dense grain that gives it exceptional strength and stability.

Oak is tannin-rich — the same compound found in red wine and tea. This natural tannin reacts with light and air over time to create the warm amber patina that customers fall in love with. Unlike other woods, oak patina is prized, not problematic.

The open grain of oak is visually striking and tactilely interesting — each plank is unique. No two Ethnicraft oak pieces are identical. This is not a defect. It is the signature of natural material.

1,360Janka hardness (lbf)
720kg/m³ density
50+Years lifespan

Selling tip: "Oak is the furniture material your grandchildren will thank you for. It only gets richer."

Teak +

Tectona grandis · Southeast Asian Heritage

Nature's waterproof. The original outdoor wood — equally stunning inside.

Teak is remarkable because it is essentially self-protecting. Its fibres are naturally saturated with silica crystals and rubber-like oils that repel moisture, resist insects, and prevent the cracking that destroys lesser woods. Shipbuilders used teak for centuries for exactly this reason.

Ethnicraft sources plantation teak — meaning trees are grown specifically for harvest in managed Indonesian forests, not cleared from ancient rainforest. This is a critical selling point with sustainability-conscious buyers.

Teak's colour ranges from golden honey when freshly oiled to a distinguished silver-grey when left untreated outdoors. Both are beautiful. Both are intentional. The customer gets to choose their aesthetic.

2,330Janka hardness (lbf)
630kg/m³ density
75+Years lifespan

Selling tip: "Teak is the only wood that genuinely requires almost no maintenance. It protects itself."

Mahogany +

Swietenia macrophylla · Warmth & Refinement

Warm reddish tones, exceptional stability, and a smoothness that's almost silky.

Mahogany has been the wood of choice for fine furniture makers for centuries — from 18th-century cabinetmakers to contemporary designers. Its warm reddish-brown tones and exceptionally fine, straight grain give finished pieces an almost sculptural quality.

What makes Mahogany technically exceptional is its dimensional stability. It moves very little with changes in humidity — meaning joints stay tight, surfaces stay flat, and doors stay true. For precise designs and complex joinery, Mahogany is the craftsman's wood.

Ethnicraft uses sustainably sourced African Mahogany, which has similar properties to its South American counterpart and is certified through responsible forestry programs.

900Janka hardness (lbf)
545kg/m³ density
100+Year potential

Selling tip: "Mahogany is the wood of Stradivarius violins and 18th-century desks. It ages with extraordinary grace."

Sycamore +

Acer pseudoplatanus · Traditional Elegance

Pale, creamy, and quietly spectacular. The wood of traditional European artisanship.

Sycamore is the quiet achiever of the Ethnicraft wood range. Pale cream to off-white in colour, with a fine, even grain, it provides a bright, contemporary aesthetic that works beautifully in modern interiors that lean towards natural tones.

In traditional European craftsmanship, sycamore was reserved for the finest furniture and musical instruments — particularly violin backs and piano keyboards — where its uniform texture, smooth surface, and ability to hold precise details were invaluable.

Sycamore brightens interiors. Where oak adds warmth and depth, sycamore adds light. For retailers with customers in smaller spaces or those seeking a Scandinavian aesthetic, sycamore is the perfect recommendation.

770Janka hardness (lbf)
625kg/m³ density
40+Years lifespan

Selling tip: "Sycamore has been used in Stradivarius violins and the finest European furniture for 300 years. It's the wood of heirlooms."

Chapter Three

From Forest to Factory

Every piece of Ethnicraft furniture begins with a transparent chain. Know the story — because your customers will ask.

In 2023, the EU introduced the EUDR — the EU Deforestation Regulation. It requires every piece of wood furniture sold in Europe to be traceable back to its forest of origin. No exceptions. No shortcuts.

Ethnicraft was already ahead of this. For years, the company has mapped its wood supply chains using satellite imagery, geolocation data, and certified sourcing partnerships. This isn't compliance for compliance's sake — it is a fundamental part of who Ethnicraft is.

When a customer asks "where does this wood come from?" — you have a complete, honest answer. That answer builds trust. That trust builds sales. That sale builds a relationship that lasts decades.

"We don't just know where our wood comes from. We know which forest. Which hectare. Which tree."
1

Certified Forest Origin

Wood is sourced only from forests certified under responsible forestry programs (FSC or PEFC). Each forest is geo-referenced — satellite-verified as deforestation-free.

2

Chain of Custody Documentation

From sawmill to shipping container, every step is documented. EUDR-compliant Due Diligence Statements (DDS) accompany every shipment.

3

Kiln Drying & Quality Control

Timber is kiln-dried to precise moisture levels (6–8%) before production. This prevents future cracking and ensures dimensional stability across all climates.

4

Production & Finishing

From raw plank to finished product. Multiple production stages, multiple human inspections. No automation replaces the craftsman's eye.

5

Arrives in Your Showroom

Each piece carries its provenance. You can tell the complete story of the wood's journey — from forest to your showroom floor.

Chapter Four

The Healthy Home

What's inside your furniture matters as much as what's on the surface. Especially for the air your customers breathe.

Most people don't think about what their furniture is emitting into their home's air. They should. And as a retailer, this is one of the most powerful — and underused — arguments for solid wood.

🌿

Zero Formaldehyde

MDF and chipboard are bonded with urea-formaldehyde resins. These off-gas for years inside homes. Solid wood contains no synthetic binders. Zero formaldehyde. Full stop.

✓

PFAS-Free Finishes

PFAS ("forever chemicals") are found in many surface treatments. Ethnicraft uses only PFAS-free, natural oil-based or water-based lacquer finishes — safe for the home, safe for the planet.

🏠

Naturally Regulating

Wood is hygroscopic — it naturally absorbs and releases moisture, helping to regulate indoor humidity levels. It acts as a passive climate control system in the home.

Ethnicraft furniture in a designed living environment — natural materials in natural settings.

Wood has been part of human homes for as long as humans have had homes. It has been used for furniture, tools, musical instruments, wine barrels, and kitchen utensils — the same material in the Stradivarius violin is in the Ethnicraft dining table in your showroom.

This is not just a material story. It is a wellbeing story. And wellbeing stories sell beautifully in today's market, where customers are thinking harder than ever about the environments they live in.

"When you choose solid wood, you choose a material with no hidden chemistry. What you see is what you get — and what you breathe."
