02 — Art Direction

Seize Your
Moment

2024. In a world tethered to technology and hurried schedules, our bodies ache for the simple joys of the natural world. This campaign was built from that tension — Ethnicraft as the place to pause, untether, and feel the sun on your skin. Photography: Laure Van Hijfte. Location: South of France.

The brief started with a universal truth: in a world tethered to technology, digital to-do lists, and hurried schedules, our bodies and hearts ache for something simpler. The feeling of grass on your fingertips. A breeze in your hair. The wild thing that stirs in us the moment we step outside. "Seize Your Moment" was built from that tension — not an aspirational lifestyle fantasy, but a genuine invitation to untether. To feel the sun on your skin. To sit down and actually be somewhere.

Ethnicraft's outdoor collection becomes the permission slip — the perfect place to pause and reconnect with the natural world. I translated this into a campaign that felt lived, not staged. Not furniture posed in a landscape, but furniture in genuine dialogue with it. I developed the full creative brief, defined the location strategy, and art directed the shoot with Laure Van Hijfte in the South of France. The light, the heat, the open terrain — all chosen to make the feeling tangible. Model casting was for natural presence: people who look like they've already arrived, not people performing leisure.

03 — Art Direction & Launch Strategy

Ethnicraft
Exposed

A three-edition Fall 2025 editorial platform — stripping the furniture of lifestyle context and letting material, form, and craft speak on their own.

I developed "Ethnicraft Exposed" as a counterpoint to the brand's usual lifestyle photography. The concept: remove the room, remove the props, remove the context. Give the product raw light and direct composition. The result forces the viewer to read a piece of furniture as a designed object — not a room accessory. "Our most trusted pieces don't stand still. They continue to evolve with purpose."

The format ran across three Fall 2025 editions, each themed around a different dimension of the collection. Collection Extensions introduced new finishes and formats to existing hero pieces — N701 sofa in chestnut leather, Bok in cognac leather and teak brown varnish, PI extensions, Elements tables in microcement. Craftsmanship Novelties focused on woodworking: "where timeless skill meets modern expression" — Pillar sideboards in wild brown teak, Brutalist pieces by Alain van Havre, Flow series in solid mahogany. Design Shift Additions spotlighted practical refinements: "not all invention looks futuristic. Some simply feel right the moment you live with them." I developed all three concepts, directed photography, produced every channel asset, and launched the B2B hub alongside the campaign.

04 — Creative Direction & Launch Strategy

PI —
Growing
Roots

April 2025. Full multi-channel launch for the PI collection — introducing the new PI dining chair and completing the collection's dining lineup. Six weeks, five stakeholder groups, one coordinated release.

PI stands for "polished imperfect." The collection was created by Belgian designer Alain van Havre, whose design process begins in nature — weekly walks through forests, time on the coast. Every PI piece carries that source: the dining chair's silhouette echoes a tree, its seat the erosion marks of a polished stone, its arms the lightness of a feather. The "Growing Roots" campaign name came directly from the collection's philosophy — design that finds its character through connection to the natural world.

PI Growing Roots is the most complete demonstration of what I do at Ethnicraft. It is a mini brand launch: concept, shoot, event, PR, social, retail, B2C, and B2B — all sequenced to a single release window. I challenged the usual single-channel approach and built a fully parallel multi-track system across five stakeholder groups, each receiving tailored content. I coordinated the photoshoot (set creation, product styling, video direction), managed the PR event for trade and press, and produced every channel asset — individually briefed for B2B retail partners, B2B trade partners (residential and commercial), and end consumers.

05 — Process

How I Build
Campaigns

My creative process

Every campaign at Ethnicraft runs through one person — from the first brief to the final asset delivered to a distributor in Singapore. That means creative director, art director, production manager, copywriter, and brand guardian, often at the same time.

ADW preparation, spring novelties, catalogue production, and a B2B campaign can all be live simultaneously — each at a different production stage, each requiring a different set of decisions. This is the permanent condition of the role, not the exception. I developed the systems to run it.

06 — Brand Systems

The Infrastructure

The strategic half of the work

Campaigns are visible. The systems that make them consistent across 80+ markets are not. I built the frameworks that let distributors, PR agencies, and trade partners worldwide all speak from the same brand. These are not support materials — they are the brand at scale.

A photographer in Japan maintains the same standard as a shoot in Belgium because the guidelines say exactly how. A new sales rep in the US understands the material story because the Academy was designed for it. The Sofa Configurator closes sales a brochure cannot. Each system required months of strategic thinking before a single page was designed.

I developed the complete brand identity system and deployed it to 80+ markets, 6 PR agencies, and all photographic and print production. Two versions: commercial (for trade partners) and internal (full channel and format logic). I also developed a parallel rebrand proposal — a strategic case for the next identity evolution — and presented it to senior management.
